Welcome to Marasy!

Marasy is located in the Al Bateen district of Abu Dhabi. It is within walking distance of some of the UAE’s most popular tourist attractions including the Abu Dhabi Corniche, The Emirates Palace Hotel, and Qasr al Watan. Cultural attractions include The Zayed Heritage Village and Etihad Modern Art Gallery. Of course, if you venture out to the surrounding city, you can also visit the Louvre Abu Dhabi, Qasr al Hosn, and The Sheikh Zayed Grand Mosque, just to name a few of the many attractions Abu Dhabi has to offer.

Living

This small waterfront community, whose prime focus is to promote tourism and public recreation, also has a wide selection of residential properties. An assortment of studios, as well as one, two, and three-bedroom apartments are available in the several different complexes. There are also seafront villas for rent with up to four bedrooms and five bathrooms. Marasy also has a variety of grocery stores, as well as medical and business offices.

Dining

Marasy has a wide variety of restaurants to choose from. Whether you need a cup of coffee, a café for breakfast, a trendy lunch spot, or one of Abu Dhabi’s finest dining experiences, Marasy has it all. Some of the most popular places include Tasha’s, % Arabica, and Brunch & Cake. No matter what you are in the mood for, Marasy has you covered.

Abu Dhabi's Rich History*

Ancient settlements of the region can be traced back as far as over 5000 years ago, and have been found around parts of Abu Dhabi, such as Jebel Hafeet near Al Ain and on the island of Umm al Nar. Abu Dhabi’s most significant settlement was those of the Bani Yas Bedouin tribe which were located by the coast around the 16th Century. The discovery of fresh water led the tribe to relocate to the island, which was more fertile with large quantities of wildlife. Soon, the ruling Al Nahyan family decided to flee to the island as well.

Under the rule of Sheikh Zayed bin Khalifa, Abu Dhabi developed through the trading of pearls, and in 1892 the very first exclusive treaty, linking Abu Dhabi with Great Britain, was signed. The emirates location was seen as a tremendous strategic convenience connecting it with India and the east, and it was established as the Trucial Coast.

Abu Dhabi’s wealth was looking on the upside until it was severely hit by the intervention of Japan’s pearl industry and also by the 1930’s global recession. Furthermore, Sheik Zayed bin Khalifa passed away, which added more uncertainty to Abu Dhabi’s prosperity.

However, this was not the end of Abu Dhabi’s fortunes. In 1939 Sheikh Shakhbut bin Sultan Al Nahyan granted petroleum concession to the Trucial Coast Development Oil Company, (renamed the Abu Dhabi Petroleum Company, ADPC, in 1962) in a bid to search for vast oil reserves. In 1958, huge offshore oil reserves were discovered, and a year later onshore reserves were also found. It was not until 1962 that the oil exports began, leading Abu Dhabi on to the road of unbelievable wealth.

In 1966 Sheik Zayed bin Sultan Al Nahyan became the new ruler of Abu Dhabi, and two years later he formed the federation of the United Arab Emirates, which was in response to the British threatening to withdraw from the region by the end of 1971. The ruler of Abu Dhabi realised that his connection with Britain was vital to maintain a strong position in the oil industry. Sheikh Zayed bin Sultan Al Nahayan was elected as the first president and with the assistance of the British, started to carry out his vision of developing the country with the advantage of increased oil revenues.

Sheikh Zayed is known as the ‘Father of the Nation’ due to his significant developments of the city of Abu Dhabi. Unfortunately, he passed away in 2004. His son Sheik Khalifa bin Zayed Al Nahyan was elected as president and has made sure he continued his father’s legacy.

Sheik Khalifa has invested oil reserves sensibly to boost tourism figures. With the outstanding determination of the Al Nahyan family reign, anything is possible. Key fascinating and impressive developments have been implemented, such as Saadiyat Island and Al Grum Resort to attract visitors from all over the globe.

Abu Dhabi, the capital of the UAE, has come a long way; rapidly re-constructing itself into a beautiful city, with extraordinary skyscrapers, shopping malls, top quality hotels and picturesque gardens. It is undoubtedly very different from what it was 50 years ago, but its history still remains a prominent factor in its path to success. (*See Attributions Page)
